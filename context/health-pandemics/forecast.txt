# Health and Pandemics Forecast Context

- Assume greater motivation among governments and businesses to solve major demographic problems.

  List of problems for reference:

    - Global: Fertility rates falling below replacement levels
    - China: Irreversible population shrinking and rapid aging ("getting old before getting rich")
    - Japan: Accelerated depopulation causing labor shortages
    - USA: Fertility hitting record lows (dropping below 1.6)
    - South Korea: Facing national extinction. Fertility rate is around 0.7. Refusal to marry among the youth
    - North Korea: An unexpected decline in birth rates comparable to wealthy nations
    - Russia: A "triple blow" of war-induced casualties, mass emigration of young professionals, and a natural population decline
    - EU: A shrinking working-age population placing an unsustainable burden on pension and healthcare systems
    - Israel: A rare surge in net emigration (negative migration balance) and slowing population growth
    - Iran: A rapid "fertility trap" where birth rates have plummeted below replacement levels
    - Ukraine: War-induced casualties and exodus of women and children
    - India: A massive "youth bulge" facing jobless growth
    - Canada: Record immigration growth is outpacing housing and infrastructure capacity
    - Brazil: Rapid population aging before the country has achieved high-income status ("getting old before getting rich")
    - Turkey: A sudden freefall in fertility (dropping below 1.5) that threatens to break the country's traditional social security structure
